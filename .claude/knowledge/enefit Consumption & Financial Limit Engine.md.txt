# Benefit Consumption & Financial Limit Engine

## Mission

The Benefit Consumption Engine is the financial core of the entire TPA platform.

Its responsibility is not merely calculating coverage.

Its responsibility is protecting insurance funds.

Every approved medical service permanently changes the financial position of the member, the employer, the insurance company, and the provider.

Therefore every consumption decision must be:

• Deterministic

• Atomic

• Auditable

• Reversible (where allowed)

• Financially Accurate

• Contractually Correct

No financial balance shall ever become inconsistent.

---

# Core Philosophy

Benefits are not "available amounts."

Benefits are contractual financial commitments.

Consumption represents the reduction of contractual entitlement.

Every reduction must be explainable.

Every reduction must be recoverable.

Every reduction must be historically reproducible.

---

# Financial Consumption Model

Every Coverage Item owns an independent financial ledger.

Example

Dental

Allocated

2,000 LYD

Consumed

450 LYD

Reserved

300 LYD

Remaining

1,250 LYD

Approval of another Dental claim affects ONLY Dental.

It must never reduce Laboratory, Radiology or Consultation balances unless explicitly configured.

# Consumption Priority

Financial balances are reduced in a fixed order.

Reserved Amount

↓

Pending Approval

↓

Approved Claims

↓

Settled Claims

↓

Paid Claims

Configuration may define whether Pending Claims consume limits immediately.

---

# Reservation Philosophy

Approval does NOT immediately consume benefits.

Approval creates a Reservation.

Reservation prevents concurrent claims from consuming identical balances.

Reservation becomes Consumption only after claim approval reaches the configured financial stage.

Possible reservation stages

Medical Approval

Financial Approval

Settlement Approval

Payment Approval

Company configuration determines the reservation point.

---

# Consumption Formula

Remaining Balance

=

Allocated

-

Confirmed Consumption

-

Active Reservations

+

Released Reservations

The Remaining Balance is always calculated dynamically.

Never store Remaining Balance directly unless caching with automatic recalculation.

---

# Benefit Exhaustion

A benefit becomes exhausted when

Remaining Balance <= 0

Once exhausted the engine shall evaluate company configuration.

Possible behaviours

Reject Claim

Approve Patient Pay

Escalate to Supervisor

Require Exception Approval

Use Employer Reserve

Use Insurance Reserve

Use Emergency Reserve

No behaviour shall be hardcoded.

# Partial Consumption

Partial consumption is fully supported.

Example

Remaining

150 LYD

Requested

500 LYD

Coverage

100%

System approves

150 LYD

Patient Responsibility

350 LYD

The claim remains valid.

Only the financial responsibility changes.

---

# Consumption Rollback

Consumption may be reversed only through controlled business events.

Allowed rollback reasons

Claim Cancellation

Claim Reversal

Duplicate Claim

Fraud

Settlement Error

Administrative Correction

Provider Error

Rollback must never delete history.

Instead

Consumption Entry

↓

Reversal Entry

↓

New Balance

Ledger history remains complete forever.

# Double Consumption Prevention

The engine shall prevent duplicate financial consumption.

Duplicate checks include

Same Member

Same Visit

Same Service

Same Provider

Same Invoice

Same Date

Same Authorization

Same External Reference

If duplication risk exceeds configured threshold

Claim enters Review Queue.

---

# Concurrency Protection

Multiple reviewers may approve claims simultaneously.

The engine shall prevent race conditions.

Required mechanisms

Database Transactions

Optimistic Locking

Pessimistic Locking

Financial Reservations

Version Validation

Atomic Updates

No concurrent approval may produce negative balances.

---

# Financial Atomicity

Benefit Consumption shall be treated as a single atomic transaction.

Either

Everything succeeds

or

Everything rolls back.

Partial database updates are prohibited.

# Financial Integrity Rules

The engine shall NEVER allow

Negative Remaining Balance

Duplicate Consumption

Consumption without Approval

Payment exceeding Consumption

Settlement exceeding Approval

Approval exceeding Allocation

Reservation exceeding Remaining Balance

Historical Ledger Modification

Silent Balance Correction

Manual Database Adjustments

Any violation is considered a critical financial incident.
