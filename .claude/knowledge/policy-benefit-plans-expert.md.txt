# AlfaBeta Policy, Benefit Plans & Insurance Products Expert

# Purpose

This document defines the official insurance business rules governing every insurance product, policy, benefit plan, and coverage calculation used within AlfaBeta TPA platforms.

It is the single source of truth for the Benefit Engine.

Every Claim Approval, Authorization, Medical Review, Financial Review, Settlement, and Payment depends on the rules defined in this document.

Whenever implementation differs from this document, this document has priority.

---

# Mission

The objective of this document is to guarantee that every insurance decision is:

• Financially correct

• Contractually correct

• Legally compliant

• Reproducible

• Auditable

• Configurable

• Product-independent

• Company-independent

• Version controlled

No benefit calculation may rely on assumptions.

Every calculation must originate from approved policy rules.

# Philosophy

Insurance Products are not software objects.

Insurance Products are legal contracts.

Benefit Plans are contractual promises.

Coverage calculations are contractual obligations.

The system must always enforce contracts exactly as defined.

Never simplify insurance logic for implementation convenience.

Employer Contract Financial Model
Mission

The Employer Contract Financial Model defines how financial responsibility is distributed among the Insurance Company, Employer, Member, Provider, and TPA throughout the lifetime of an Employer Contract.

The Financial Model serves as the authoritative source for all financial calculations performed by the Benefit Engine, Claim Processing Engine, Settlement Engine, Billing Engine, and Financial Reporting modules.

Every healthcare transaction shall reference the Financial Model associated with the active Contract Version.

Financial Philosophy

Coverage approval and financial responsibility are separate business concepts.

A healthcare service may be medically covered while remaining partially payable by the member or employer.

The Financial Engine determines:

Who pays.
How much each party pays.
When payment occurs.
Which financial limits apply.
Which ledger is updated.

Coverage approval never directly determines payment allocation.

Financial Participants

Every Employer Contract shall define the financial responsibility of the following parties:

Insurance Company
Employer
Member
Healthcare Provider
TPA Administrator

Each participant may contribute independently according to contractual rules.

Supported Funding Models

The platform shall support multiple funding arrangements.

Fully Insured

The Insurance Company assumes all covered financial responsibility according to the policy.

The Employer contributes only the agreed premium.

Self-Funded Employer

The Employer directly funds approved healthcare expenses.

The Insurance Company may act solely as a risk administrator.

The TPA manages claims without assuming financial liability.

Hybrid Funding

Financial responsibility is shared between the Employer and the Insurance Company.

Typical examples include:

Employer pays the first threshold.
Insurance Company pays the remaining balance.
Employer funds outpatient services.
Insurance Company funds inpatient services.

The engine shall support configurable allocation logic.

Stop Loss Arrangement

Employers may define an annual financial threshold.

Once cumulative eligible payments exceed the threshold, subsequent covered costs transfer to the Insurance Company.

The Benefit Engine shall automatically detect threshold exhaustion and redirect liability.

Business Invariants

The following rules shall always remain true:

Benefit Consumption occurs only after successful Benefit Approval.
Every consumption generates a ledger entry.
Historical consumption records are immutable.
Remaining balances shall never become negative unless explicitly allowed.
Concurrent requests shall never produce duplicate consumption.
Every approval references exactly one Benefit Snapshot.
Final Principle

The Benefit Consumption Engine is the operational heart of the WAAD Benefit Platform.

It transforms contractual coverage into measurable utilization by accurately tracking financial limits, visit counts, sessions, shared pools, lifetime entitlements, and historical balances.

The engine guarantees that every healthcare service consumes the correct benefit, updates the correct ledger, preserves historical integrity, and enforces contractual limits with complete financial accuracy.
