# Coverage Rules & Benefit Engine Decision Architecture

## Mission

Coverage Rules represent the contractual intelligence of the entire insurance platform.

Every medical service requested by a Provider shall be evaluated against these rules before any financial commitment is made.

No claim shall ever bypass the Benefit Engine.

Every approved amount, rejected amount, patient responsibility, insurer responsibility, authorization request, or denial must originate from the Coverage Rules defined herein.

Coverage Rules are immutable business contracts.

Software must obey them exactly.

---

# Benefit Engine Philosophy

The Benefit Engine never asks:

"Can this claim be paid?"

Instead it asks:

"According to the active insurance contract, what exactly is the member entitled to receive at this moment?"

The answer must always be deterministic.

Running the same calculation twice with identical inputs shall always produce identical outputs.

# Remaining Balance

Approval depends on remaining balance rather than original allocation.

Remaining Balance =

Allocated Benefit

minus

Previously Approved

minus

Pending Reserved Amount

minus

Settlements in Progress

Pending approvals shall reserve financial capacity to prevent double consumption.

---

# Financial Reservation

Immediately after preliminary approval

the requested benefit amount becomes Reserved.

Reservation prevents two simultaneous claims consuming the same remaining balance.

Reservation is released only when

Claim Cancelled

Claim Rejected

Settlement Completed

Reservation Expired

This rule is mandatory to eliminate race conditions.

---

# Partial Coverage

The engine must support partial approvals.

Example

Remaining annual balance

120 LYD

Requested service

300 LYD

Coverage

100%

Insurance pays

120 LYD

Patient pays

180 LYD

The claim continues.

It is never rejected merely because the balance is insufficient.

This behaviour may be configured per insurance company.

---

# Benefit Exhaustion

When Remaining Balance reaches zero

the engine shall

Reject future consumption

or

Approve patient-pay only

depending on company configuration.

The reviewer shall receive a warning indicating

Benefit Exhausted

Remaining Balance

Consumed Amount

Original Allocation

Date Exhausted

Number of Previous Uses

The user must understand exactly why approval failed.
