# Benefit Consumption & Financial Ledger

## Mission

The Benefit Consumption & Financial Ledger is the financial integrity engine of the WAAD platform.

Its responsibility is to ensure that every healthcare service consumes insurance benefits accurately, consistently, and only once.

Every financial movement must be completely traceable.

No benefit may disappear.

No balance may be created artificially.

No financial event may occur without a corresponding ledger record.

---

# Philosophy

Coverage determines

"What may be consumed."

The Ledger determines

"What has actually been consumed."

Coverage Rules never modify balances.

Only Ledger Transactions modify balances.

---

# Single Source of Truth

Benefit balances shall never be calculated from claims alone.

They shall always be calculated from the Financial Ledger.

Remaining Balance

=

Allocated Benefit

-

Committed Consumption

+

Reversals

+

Adjustments

Historical claims are evidence.

Ledger entries are accounting truth.

# Reservation Principle

Before approval

Benefits are Reserved.

Reservation temporarily blocks the remaining balance.

Reservation prevents

Concurrent approvals

Duplicate consumption

Race conditions

Financial inconsistencies

Reservation does not reduce the permanent balance.

---

# Commitment Principle

Benefits become officially consumed only when

Claim Approved

OR

Authorization Finalized

OR

Settlement Confirmed

Before commitment

Benefits remain recoverable.

---

# Reversal Principle

If a committed transaction is cancelled

Reverse

the exact consumed amount.

Never create a negative transaction manually.

Always generate a Reversal Ledger Entry.

# System Invariants

The following rules must always remain true.

A benefit can only be consumed once.

A reservation cannot exceed remaining balance.

A reversal must reference an existing transaction.

Consumption cannot become negative.

Historical ledger entries cannot be modified.

Every balance change creates a ledger record.

Every financial event is auditable.

Every ledger entry belongs to exactly one business event.
