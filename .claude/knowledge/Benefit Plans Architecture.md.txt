# Benefit Plans Architecture

Benefit Plans define the medical and financial entitlements granted to insured members.

A Benefit Plan is not merely a list of covered services.

It is a complete rule engine describing how coverage is calculated, consumed, restricted, authorized, settled, and audited.

Every insurance product references one or more Benefit Plans.

Every member ultimately receives coverage through an assigned Benefit Plan.

---

# Benefit Plan Philosophy

Benefit Plans must satisfy four principles.

Consistency

Every member with identical eligibility must receive identical calculations.

Configurability

Business users must configure plans without changing application code.

Versionability

Benefit Plans evolve through versions while preserving historical calculations.

Auditability

Every coverage decision must be explainable years later.

# Rule Priority

When multiple rules apply simultaneously, the engine evaluates in order.

Claim Override

↓

Member Override

↓

Employer Override

↓

Benefit Plan

↓

Insurance Product

↓

System Default

The first applicable rule becomes authoritative.

---

# Plan Versioning

Benefit Plans never change in place.

Every significant modification creates a new version.

Examples

Annual Limit changed

Copayment changed

Coverage Percentage changed

Waiting Period modified

Network modified

Authorization rules changed

Historical claims always reference the version active at the service date.
